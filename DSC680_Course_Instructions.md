## Course Structure
There's only this course left and it's broken down in a more flexible way compared to previous courses. There are 3, 4-week blocks - think of them as sprints. You have some check-ins along the way, but for the most part, all the work needs to be completed by the end of the 4th week. This gives you plenty of time to focus on completing the work vs worrying about a weekly deadline.

## Course Requirements

### 1. Final Projects (3)
You must complete 3 projects. Each project has the following milestones:
- **Milestone 1 (Week 1)**: Project Proposal
- **Milestone 2 (Week 3)**: Draft White Paper (3-5 pages)
- **Milestone 3 (Week 4)**: Final White Paper and Recorded Presentation

### 2. Portfolio
You'll be asked to complete certain tasks for your portfolio, but most of the content should come from previous courses. Create a GitHub repository to showcase your work.

### 3. Discussion/Participation
Take this opportunity in your final course to network with peers, solicit feedback on your portfolio, resume, and final projects. Another great discussion opportunity is to talk about potential jobs and interview tips/tricks.

Requirements:
- 10 posts every week
- Be active at least a couple of days each week

### 4. Program Assessment
This is pass/fail, meaning all you have to do is submit.

## Final Project Grading Criteria

Each project will be evaluated based on:

1. **Proposal (Milestone 1)**
   - Clear topic definition
   - Well-defined business problem and research questions
   - Appropriate datasets identified
   - Comprehensive methods outlined
   - Ethical considerations addressed
   - Challenges/issues identified
   - Proper references included

2. **White Paper Draft (Milestone 2)**
   - Progress on data collection and analysis
   - Initial findings and visualizations
   - Structured document with clear sections
   - Application of appropriate methods
   - Preliminary conclusions

3. **Final Submission (Milestone 3)**
   - Comprehensive white paper (quality of writing, organization, completeness)
   - Effective presentation (clarity, visual appeal, delivery)
   - Technical proficiency (appropriate use of methods and tools)
   - Insights and conclusions (depth of analysis, value of findings)
   - Professional presentation of results

## Tips for Success
- Choose topics that genuinely interest you
- Ensure your data is accessible before committing to a project
- Consider the feasibility of completing the analysis within the timeframe
- Think about how you can tell a compelling, jargon-free story with your data
- Focus on providing useful insights for those in your chosen domain
- Start early on each project to allow time for revisions
- Leverage skills and knowledge from your previous courses
- Create a professional portfolio that showcases your best work
