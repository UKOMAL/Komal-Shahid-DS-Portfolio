Your point about transitioning into data science from a non-technical field really stood out to me. I think the people who come in with domain expertise — whether that is healthcare, finance, or operations — often end up being stronger analysts than those of us who started purely on the technical side, because they already know which questions are actually worth answering.

The challenge I see for people making that shift is bridging the communication gap in reverse: learning enough of the vocabulary to work with engineering teams and read the literature, without losing the business intuition that makes the work meaningful. The MSDS program pushes both directions at once, which is probably why it feels intensive.

For DSC680 specifically, I think the three-project structure is well-designed for exactly that reason — you have to make domain choices, not just technical ones. What domain are you thinking of targeting for your projects? I ended up with workplace mental health partly because the data was clean and public, but also because it touches organizational behavior, which has real applications in HR tech and policy.

Would love to hear more about your project direction.
