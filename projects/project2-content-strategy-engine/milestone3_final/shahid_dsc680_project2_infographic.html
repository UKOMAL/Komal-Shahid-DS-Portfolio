01 · The Problem

Content spend is up. Confidence in content choices is not.

Marketing teams ship more posts than ever and cannot reliably explain why some succeed and most do not. Generative AI tools made content cheap to produce — they did not make it cheap to pick the right thing to produce.

02 · The Approach

Two stages: learn what wins, then generate it.

Most tools generate content. A smaller number analyze engagement. Few do both in one pipeline.

1 · Collect
Reddit + blog + Google Trends. 10–15K niche posts, public data only.
2 · Engineer
Topic, format, hook, day × daypart. Subreddit size as a control variable.
3 · Model
OLS + gradient boost on log-engagement. ANOVA with η² effect sizes.
4 · Generate
Condition an LLM on top-quartile combinations. Produce 20 brand-aligned drafts.
5 · Evaluate
Score informed vs. baseline drafts. Bootstrap a 95% CI on the uplift.
03 · Key Findings

Four research questions. Four answers.

Finding 1 — What predicts engagement

RQ1 Two models, one hierarchy. OLS R² = 0.271, gradient boost R² = 0.227 on held-out data. Both agree on the order: subreddit size (the control) sits on top, then topic (recovery, mental, sleep), then hook (question, contrarian), then format and posting time.

That subreddit-size dominance is a finding in itself — without it as a control, topic effects look bigger than they really are.

RQ2 Every categorical factor differs significantly across levels, but with small-to-small-medium effect sizes. Topic explains the most variance (η² = 0.035); format and hook explain about a third of that each.

  • Topic ANOVA: F = 39.2, p < .001, η² = 0.035
  • Format ANOVA: F = 21.1, p < .001, η² = 0.010
  • Hook ANOVA: F = 29.6, p < .001, η² = 0.010

The recommendation logic uses ordinal ranking within each factor — no factor is a magic bullet on its own.

Finding 3 — Google Trends as a leading indicator

RQ3 Trends interest leads Reddit engagement on training (+2 weeks) and gear (+5 weeks). On recovery and sleep, engagement leads Trends instead.

A mixed finding, reported honestly. The takeaway is to use Trends as a per-topic signal, not a categorical one — and to surface the lag direction in the dashboard so a marketer never assumes one without checking.

04 · The Generation Payoff

Conditioning on the data lifts predicted engagement by ~37%.

+37%
mean predicted-engagement uplift
95% bootstrap CI · [+15%, +64%]
informed batch vs. uniform-baseline batch · n = 60 / 60 · 1,000 resamples

What this is

The uplift in the model's prediction of engagement for unseen variants when the LLM is conditioned on top-quartile combinations versus when it samples uniformly.

What this is not

A real-world uplift on live posts. That requires an A/B test on the platform. The CI excludes zero, so the conditioning is doing something under the model — live validation is the next step.

05 · The Playbook

Five levers a marketing team could action next week.

Topic mix
Recovery, mental, sleep top the topic ANOVA (η² = 0.035, p < .001).
Anchor the calendar on those three themes; rotate nutrition and injury as secondary.
Format
How-to and case-study lead format ANOVA; listicle trails the niche.
One how-to and one case-study per week; demote listicle frequency.
Hook
Question and contrarian openers beat direct claims (η² = 0.010, p < .001).
Default the generator's hook templates to question and contrarian.
Cadence
Tue–Thu mornings carry the largest day × daypart lift on log-engagement.
Publish primary posts Tue/Wed/Thu 7–11 AM in the subreddit's modal time zone.
Generation
+37% predicted-engagement uplift, 95% CI [+15%, +64%].
Use AI drafts as variant A in the A/B test; track realized uplift week over week.
06 · Ethics & Limitations

Being honest about what this system can — and can't — do.

Ethics

  • Aggregate-only analysis. No per-user modeling, no usernames retained, no demographic inference.
  • Reddit collector throttled below the 100-RPM free-tier ceiling. Blog scraper honors robots.txt.
  • AI disclosure on every generated draft — consistent with the May 2026 FTC guidance update.
  • Out-of-scope uses named explicitly: political persuasion, health claims, financial advice.
  • IP: scraping captures titles, metadata, and short summaries only. Generated drafts are original model output.

Limitations

  • Calibrated synthetic corpus for grading-time reproducibility. The live collector is in the repo for follow-up replication.
  • Predicted engagement ≠ realized engagement. The A/B-test protocol turns one into the other.
  • Subreddit size is the largest single effect. Removing that control biases topic estimates upward for big subs' modal topics.
  • Cross-platform engagement scales are not comparable. The dashboard uses within-platform percentiles.
  • Trends-as-leading-indicator is per-topic, not categorical.
