Hi everyone,

My name is Komal Shahid, and I am finishing my M.S. in Data Science at Bellevue University through DSC680. Professionally I work as an AI Engineer, ML Engineer, and Data Scientist. My focus has been on building systems that are not just accurate but explainable and deployable — models with clear documentation, interpretability layers, and responsible design built in from the start rather than added after.

Through the MSDS program I have worked on three recurring problem types: clinical NLP (including transformer-based text classification for mental health signals), privacy-preserving learning for healthcare settings where raw data cannot be shared across institutions, and fraud detection pipelines with conservative handling of class imbalance and SHAP explanations for compliance teams. I also care about the deployment side — reproducible pipelines, containers, and lightweight APIs — because that is where models actually affect real users.

My project for this term uses the OSMI 2016 Mental Health in Tech Survey to predict treatment-seeking behavior among technology workers. I chose this topic because workplace mental health is under-analyzed compared to its organizational impact, and because the dataset is public, well-documented, and small enough that every modeling decision is visible without hiding behind scale.

Looking ahead, I am interested in roles in healthcare AI, fintech, responsible AI, and MLOps. For DSC680 specifically, I want to sharpen how I communicate technical findings to mixed audiences, and I hope to give and receive honest peer feedback on methods and presentation choices. If anyone is working in NLP, tabular ML, or privacy-preserving systems, I would genuinely like to connect.

Looking forward to learning with all of you this term.
