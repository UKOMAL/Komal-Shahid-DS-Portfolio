# Week 4 Discussion — Post 2 (Reply to Courtney Johnson): AI and Career Anxiety

**Course:** DSC680-T301 Applied Data Science  
**Author:** Komal Shahid  
**Week:** 4 | **Type:** Reply to Courtney Johnson (~200 words)

---

Courtney, your point about AI creating uncertainty around data science careers genuinely resonates with me. I have had similar thoughts while building models for this course — at what point does automation replace the judgment calls we are learning to make?

What I keep coming back to is that the data science roles being displaced tend to be the ones that were already narrow: cleaning the same format of data repeatedly, running the same query against a dashboard, generating the same weekly report. Those tasks are automatable precisely because they do not require domain knowledge or interpretive skill.

The roles that seem more resilient involve understanding why a result matters and what to do about it. Communicating findings to stakeholders who do not speak Python, recognizing when a model is technically correct but practically wrong, knowing which ethical questions to ask before deploying — those skills are harder to encode. I think the field is shifting toward hybrid roles that combine technical fluency with business and communication competence, and a graduate program like this one is actually well-positioned for that shift.

The anxiety makes sense, but I do not think it cancels out the value of what we are building here.
